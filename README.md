## Key Questions

1. Which states have the largest underserved populations affected by mental health provider shortages?

2. Which counties experience the most severe mental health provider shortages?

3. Are rural communities more affected by mental health provider shortages than urban communities?

4. Which communities face both severe shortages and large underserved populations?

5. What recommendations can help improve access to mental health care?

6. ## Key Findings

### Rural Communities Face Greater Shortage Severity

Rural communities demonstrated the highest average Mental Health Professional Shortage Area (HPSA) scores (14.81), exceeding both non-rural (14.15) and partially rural (13.75) communities. These findings suggest that rural populations may face greater barriers to accessing mental health services.

### Underserved Populations Are Concentrated in Several States

New York, Illinois, California, Nevada, and Florida represented the largest estimated underserved populations within designated mental health shortage areas. This indicates that mental health workforce shortages affect large populations across several states.

### County-Level Shortages Remain Significant

Several counties, including St. Mary Parish (LA), Lee County (GA), Bleckley County (GA), and Emery County (UT), recorded some of the highest shortage severity scores in the dataset. These areas may face substantial challenges in providing timely access to mental health care services.
